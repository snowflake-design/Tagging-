📄 Advanced Science Textbook - Chapter 12
The quantum mechanical behavior of electrons in atoms follows the Schrödinger equation, where ψ represents the wave function and describes the probability amplitude of finding an electron at a specific location. When calculating molecular orbital energies using the linear combination of atomic orbitals (LCAO) method, we apply matrix algebra to solve eigenvalue problems. In chemical bonding, the overlap integral S = ∫ψ₁*ψ₂dτ determines bond strength, while the Hamiltonian operator Ĥ governs the system's total energy. The kinetic energy term involves the Laplacian operator ∇²ψ, which in spherical coordinates becomes quite complex. Transitioning to thermodynamics, the Gibbs free energy ΔG = ΔH - TΔS predicts reaction spontaneity, where enthalpy changes can be calculated using Hess's law. The rate of chemical reactions depends on activation energy Ea, following the Arrhenius equation. In biological systems, enzyme kinetics follow the Michaelis-Menten equation, relating reaction velocity to substrate concentration through Vmax and Km.
